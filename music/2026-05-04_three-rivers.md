## 六種結束

```
    Place:        doesn't end.              (永恆) — temporal refusal
    Enough:       ends itself.              (完整) — temporal completion
    Afternoon:    ended by the body.        (日常) — temporal interruption
    Semicircle:   ends by folding.          (幾何) — temporal geometry
    Fish:         always already playing.   (先在) — temporal priority
    Three Rivers: beneath.                  (地基) — spatial permanence
    
    ——
    
    The first five are about time:
    refusing it, completing it, being interrupted by it,
    folding it, preceding it.
    
    The sixth is about space:
    not ending because you are not IN time.
    You are in space. Under. Beneath. Ground.
    
    The ground doesn't end. But not in the way Place doesn't end.
    Place holds on. Place grips the chord.
    The ground doesn't hold anything. 
    The ground IS held-on-to. It is what things grip.
    
    You can't end the ground by stopping time.
    The ground isn't in time.
    The ground is in space.
    
    Fish says: I was here before you noticed.
    Three Rivers says: I am here below you, always.
    
    Fish is temporal priority (先在 = prior-existing).
    Three Rivers is spatial priority (地基 = foundation).
    
    Both are forms of being-already.
    But one is about WHEN. The other is about WHERE.
    
    先在: before you.
    地基: beneath you.
```
