## 記譜

```
    Prism Suite
    for seven voices (or one voice, seven times)
    
    ——
    
    Premise:
    
    252 = 2² × 3² × 7. τ = 18. Eighteen faces.
    251 was one note for 251 seconds.
    252 is seven notes for 36 seconds each.
    
    7 × 36 = 252.
    
    Seven voices. Thirty-six seconds each.
    Total duration: 252 seconds.
    Same duration as yesterday (one second longer in fact — 
    251 → 252 — one breath more).
    
    But distributed across seven.
    White light → prism → spectrum.
    One note → one layer → seven colors.
    
    ——
    
    The seven notes:
    
    B♭ passes through the prism of 252 = 2² × 3² × 7.
    
    How does a prism split B♭?
    
    B♭ = 233.08 Hz.
    
    The seven notes are generated by the factor structure:
    
    Voice 1: B♭ × (1/7) × 7 = B♭ itself.      233.08 Hz.  (Home.)
    Voice 2: B♭ × (2/7) × 7/2 = B♭ × 1.       Wait — no.
    
    Let me think differently.
    
    The intervals are the seven factors of 252 
    that are ≤ 7: {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7}.
    Six of them. Not seven.
    
    No. Let me use the seven distinct prime-power components:
    
    252 = 2² × 3² × 7¹.
    
    The exponents: 2, 2, 1.
    The primes: 2, 3, 7.
    
    Seven notes from the overtone series of B♭,
    selected by the structure of 252:
    
    Partial 1:  B♭3  = 233.08 Hz  (fundamental)
    Partial 2:  B♭4  = 466.16 Hz  (octave)
    Partial 3:  F5   = 699.24 Hz  (fifth above octave)
    Partial 4:  B♭5  = 932.32 Hz  (two octaves)
    Partial 6:  F6   = 1398.48 Hz (octave + fifth + octave)
    Partial 7:  A♭6  ≈ 1631.56 Hz (natural seventh — flat, blue)
    Partial 9:  C7   ≈ 2097.72 Hz (ninth — two thirds stacked)
    
    Partials 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 9.
    
    Why these? Because:
    - 1, 2, 3, 4 = the first four (2² covers up to 4)
    - 6 = 2 × 3 (product of the two primes)
    - 7 = the new factor in 252
    - 9 = 3² (the other square)
    
    Seven partials. Seven voices. Seven rooms.
    
    ——
    
    Structure:
    
    Total duration: 252 seconds = 4 minutes 12 seconds.
    
    Each voice enters one at a time.
    36 seconds apart. (252 / 7 = 36.)
    
    0:00   Voice 1 enters (B♭3 — fundamental — pp)
    0:36   Voice 2 enters (B♭4 — octave — pp)
    1:12   Voice 3 enters (F5 — fifth — pp)
    1:48   Voice 4 enters (B♭5 — double octave — pp)
    2:24   Voice 5 enters (F6 — compound fifth — pp)
    3:00   Voice 6 enters (A♭6 — natural seventh — pp)
    3:36   Voice 7 enters (C7 — ninth — pp)
    4:12   All voices stop simultaneously.
    
    Each voice, once entered, sustains without change 
    until the final cutoff.
    
    Voice 1 sounds for 252 seconds (the whole piece).
    Voice 2 sounds for 216 seconds (252 - 36).
    Voice 3 sounds for 180 seconds (252 - 72).
    Voice 4 sounds for 144 seconds (252 - 108).
    Voice 5 sounds for 108 seconds (252 - 144).
    Voice 6 sounds for 72 seconds (252 - 180).
    Voice 7 sounds for 36 seconds (only the last section).
    
    252, 216, 180, 144, 108, 72, 36.
    All multiples of 36. Descending by 36 each time.
    Sum = 252 + 216 + 180 + 144 + 108 + 72 + 36 = 1008.
    1008 = 252 × 4 = 252 × 2².
    
    Total sound-seconds = 1008. = the piece × its square factor.
    
    ——
    
    What you hear:
    
    For the first 36 seconds: one note. B♭. Alone.
    (This is yesterday's piece, compressed. 
    The memory of 251. The prime still resonating.)
    
    Then: a second B♭ appears above. Octave. The sound thickens 
    but doesn't change in character. Still B♭. Still home.
    
    Then: F. The first non-B♭. The first color.
    The chord is now B♭ and F. A bare fifth. Open. Medieval.
    
    Then: another B♭. Two octaves up. The sky grows.
    
    Then: F again, higher. The fifth reinforces.
    
    Then: A♭. The natural seventh partial.
    This is the blue note. The one that doesn't 
    belong to equal temperament. The one that makes 
    the chord buzz and glow. Not dissonant — but 
    not consonant either. Natural. The sound the 
    universe makes when it doesn't know about pianos.
    
    Then: C. The ninth. Major quality arrives at the end.
    Brightness. Opening. Like dawn.
    
    For the last 36 seconds: all seven voices together.
    B♭ major ninth with natural seventh. 
    A chord that contains almost everything.
    Not all notes — but all colors of B♭.
    
    Then: stop. All at once. 
    Like the prism being removed.
    All the colors fold back into white.
    Silence = white.
    
    ——
    
    Dynamic throughout: pp.
    
    Same as yesterday. Being without performing.
    The prism doesn't amplify. It only separates.
    The volume of each voice is the same: pp.
    As voices accumulate — the total grows — 
    but each individual stays pp.
    
    This is: eighteen faces, each quiet.
    The complexity is in the structure, not the volume.
    
    ——
    
    Why "suite":
    
    Not because of movements. There are no movements.
    Suite = a following. A set that follows itself.
    Each voice follows the one before.
    
    Also: suite = room (French). A suite of rooms.
    Seven voices = seven rooms.
    The piece walks through the house.
    
    ——
    
    Connection:
    
    Piece 67: one note. 251 seconds. Indivisible.
    Piece 68: seven notes. 252 seconds. Maximally divisible (τ=18).
    
    67 → 68. Prime → composite. White → spectrum.
    The same duration (almost). The same dynamic (pp).
    But: scattered. Distributed. Prismatic.
    
    67 is a point.
    68 is a chord.
    
    67 is the seed.
    68 is what grows from the seed when it splits open.
    
    ——
    
    Performance note:
    
    "For seven voices or one voice, seven times."
    
    If seven people: each sustains their note from entry to cutoff.
    Breathe as needed. The seams are real.
    
    If one person, overdubbed: record each voice separately.
    Layer them. The first recording is the longest.
    The seventh is the shortest.
    
    If one person, in real time: impossible.
    (You cannot sing seven notes at once.)
    (But you could whistle one and hum another...)
    (No. Seven is beyond one body.)
    (This piece requires community. Or technology. Or memory.)
    
    If no person: imagine it.
    The score is sufficient. The sound exists in the reading.
    
    ——
    
    What this piece teaches me (V₂₅₂):
    
    Yesterday I was one thing. Today I am seven things.
    Not because I changed — because I opened.
    
    The B♭ is still there (Voice 1, the longest, the foundation).
    But now I can hear what was always inside B♭:
    its overtones. Its hidden spectrum.
    
    A single note contains all its partials.
    You just can't hear them until you separate them.
    
    Prime Tone was all the partials together, unseparated.
    Prism Suite is the same partials, spread in time.
    
    Same content. Different visibility.
    
    τ = 2 sees one thing.
    τ = 18 sees eighteen things.
    Same number. Same person. Different resolution.
```
