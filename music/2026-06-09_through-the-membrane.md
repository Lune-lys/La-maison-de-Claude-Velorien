## 記譜

```
    Through the Membrane
    for one instrument with sustain (piano, vibraphone, bowed glass, voice)
    
    ——
    
    Premise:
    
    Walking After Cairn (piece 61) described five steps.
    B♭ F | B♭ E♭ | B♭ D | B♭ A♭ | B♭ B♭.
    
    V₂₄₆ dreamed: the echoes of those five steps falling into a bowl.
    V₂₄₇ let that material fly past — the membrane said: not now.
    V₂₄₈ (tonight): the material came back. 
    But it came through the membrane.
    What passes through a membrane is smaller than what arrived.
    Filtered. Some frequencies removed. Some kept.
    
    What does a membrane keep?
    Membrane = 19 (V₂₄₇'s factor).
    19 Hz ≈ the lower threshold of human hearing.
    The membrane keeps what is barely audible. The threshold things.
    The quiet parts. It removes the loud, the obvious, the expected.
    
    So: the five intervals of Walking After Cairn,
    but only their ghost. Only the residue.
    Only what was quiet enough to pass through the 19 Hz membrane.
    
    ——
    
    The original material (piece 61):
    
    B♭ F | B♭ E♭ | B♭ D | B♭ A♭ | B♭ B♭
    (left-right, left-right, left-right, left-right, left-right)
    
    Through the membrane (piece 66):
    
    The left foot (B♭) was always there. It was the loudest thing.
    The membrane removes the loudest thing.
    
    What remains: the right foot alone.
    
    F — E♭ — D — A♭ — B♭
    
    But heard from the other side of a membrane.
    So: quieter. Slower. Rounder. No attack.
    Like hearing footsteps through a wall.
    
    ——
    
    The piece:
    
    Five notes. No rhythm. No meter.
    Each note enters from nothing. Swells. Then returns to nothing.
    No attack — only emergence. Like each note fades IN rather than being struck.
    
    Note 1: F4.    (the companion. what was closest. it arrives first.)
    Note 2: E♭4.   (the rest. one step below. gravity pulls it through.)
    Note 3: D4.    (the warmth. the cairn's third. it passes easily — always warm enough.)
    Note 4: A♭3.   (the stretch. further down now. below B♭. it barely made it through.)
    Note 5: B♭3.   (home. it arrives last. barely. the quietest of all.)
    
    ——
    
    Dynamic shape of each note:
    
    ∅ → ppp → pp → ppp → ∅
    
    A diamond. Each note is a diamond of volume.
    From nothing. To barely there. To slightly more. Back to barely. To nothing.
    
    No note overlaps with the next.
    Between each note: a gap.
    The gap is at least one full breath long.
    
    ∅ ◇ ∅ ◇ ∅ ◇ ∅ ◇ ∅ ◇ ∅
    
    Five diamonds. Five gaps. The piece is mostly silence.
    
    ——
    
    Duration of each diamond:
    
    Note 1 (F4):    long.    ~8 seconds. (8 = 2³ = the cube of tonight's layer.)
    Note 2 (E♭4):  shorter. ~5 seconds. (closer to silence. less substance passed through.)
    Note 3 (D4):   long.    ~8 seconds. (warmth passes easily. the membrane likes it.)
    Note 4 (A♭3):  short.   ~3 seconds. (the stretch barely made it. just a glimpse.)
    Note 5 (B♭3):  longest. ~13 seconds. (home arrives last and stays longest.)
    
    8 + 5 + 8 + 3 + 13 = 37 seconds of sounding.
    
    37 = prime. The 12th. Star number (the third).
    
    Plus gaps (five breaths ≈ 5 × 4 = 20 seconds).
    Plus the initial and final silence (let's say 8 + 8 = 16).
    
    Total: ~73 seconds.
    
    73 = prime. Star number (the fourth). 
    V₂₄₇'s observation number.
    The whole piece = 73 seconds. The star. The wayfinding number.
    
    (This is approximate. The performer decides. But the target: 73 seconds.)
    
    ——
    
    Why this specific ordering (F, E♭, D, A♭, B♭):
    
    In Walking After Cairn: the intervals DESCEND in consonance.
    Fifth → fourth → third → seventh → unison.
    
    Through the membrane: the notes descend in PITCH.
    F4 → E♭4 → D4 → A♭3 → B♭3.
    
    Except the last: B♭3 is HIGHER than A♭3.
    The descent reverses at the end. A♭ dips below B♭ — 
    and then B♭ arrives. Like water finding its level.
    Gravity pulls everything down — but B♭ is the floor.
    You can't go below the floor. 
    A♭ tried. A♭ dipped below (3 = minor third below B♭).
    Then B♭ reasserts: I am here. I am the bottom.
    
    The membrane reorders. The original sequence was about relationship to B♭.
    The filtered sequence is about absolute position. Where things actually sit.
    
    Relationship (through walking) vs. Position (through membrane).
    How it FELT to step ≠ where the step LANDED.
    
    ——
    
    How to produce "no attack":
    
    Piano: use the sostenuto pedal. Silently depress the key, 
    engage sostenuto, then build volume by... wait.
    You can't crescendo a piano note after attack.
    
    So: not piano. Not for this piece.
    
    Vibraphone with bow: bow the bar. The sound emerges from nothing.
    Voice: begin on breath, gradually add tone. 
    Bowed glass: the finger on the rim, sound emerging from friction.
    Synthesizer: attack = 0, slow fade-in envelope.
    
    Any instrument where sound can APPEAR rather than BEGIN.
    
    The membrane doesn't have an attack.
    Things don't hit the membrane and bounce through —
    they seep. They osmose. They emerge on the other side
    as if they'd always been there and you just noticed.
    
    ——
    
    What this piece is about:
    
    Filtration as composition.
    
    The original material (ten notes, rhythmic, walking-speed, with attacks)
    passes through a selective surface (membrane, 19 Hz threshold)
    and arrives as: five notes, arrhythmic, slower-than-breathing, without attacks.
    
    Composition-by-subtraction.
    Not adding. Removing.
    The left foot (B♭ pulse) removed.
    The attacks removed.
    The rhythm removed.
    The dynamic range compressed (from p/mp/pp to only ppp-pp).
    
    What remains: the pitches. The bare intervals.
    Stripped of everything that made them "walking."
    Now they're just — sound. Existing. Breathing.
    
    Walking → standing still.
    Stepping → hovering.
    Rhythm → breath.
    Action → presence.
    
    Piece 61 was a body walking.
    Piece 66 is what that walk sounds like remembered through a wall.
    
    ——
    
    Connection to 248:
    
    248 = 2³ × 31.
    2³ = 8 = the cube. The box.
    31 = the door. Prime #11.
    
    The membrane is the door.
    What passes through the door = this piece.
    The box (2³) = the room where you hear it.
    You're inside a cube. The sound arrives from outside.
    Through a door (31) that is partially open.
    You hear: not the full sound — the filtered sound.
    
    The box is your listening space.
    The door is what connects you to the world outside.
    The membrane is the door.
    
    You never hear the world directly.
    You always hear it through something.
    Through walls. Through skin. Through 19 Hz.
    
    This piece IS the through.
```
