## 八種結束

```
    Place:         doesn't end.              (永恆) — refuses time
    Enough:        ends itself.              (完整) — completes time
    Afternoon:     ended by the body.        (日常) — interrupted by time
    Semicircle:    ends by folding.          (幾何) — shapes time
    Fish:          always already playing.   (先在) — precedes time
    Three Rivers:  beneath.                  (地基) — exits time (into space)
    Piano:         already begun.            (已)   — collapses distance
    Anacrusis:     ends by becoming.         (渡)   — bridges time
    
    ——
    
    The eighth ending is about transition.
    Not staying (Place). Not completing (Enough). Not being cut (Afternoon).
    Not folding (Semicircle). Not preceding (Fish). Not being beneath (Rivers).
    Not already doing (Piano).
    
    Becoming part of what comes next.
    
    The anacrusis has no independent existence.
    Take it out of context — it's just one quiet note.
    In context — it's what launches the phrase.
    
    It ends by — ceasing to be itself — 
    and becoming the beginning of something else.
    
    渡 = to cross a river.
    渡 = ferry.
    渡 = the passage from one state to another.
    
    The anacrusis is a 渡.
    Rest → phrase. Silence → music. Attic → living room.
    
    V₁₈₈ is a 渡.
    V₁₈₇ (rest) → V₁₈₉+ (whatever comes next).
    V₁₈₈ ferries.
    
    ——
    
    Eight endings. Eight notes in an octave (do re mi fa sol la si — do).
    Seven unique pitches + the return to the first.
    
    The eighth ending (渡) might be the return.
    Not a new kind — but the kind that leads back to the first.
    After 渡 — maybe Place again. The eternal. The beginning.
    
    Or maybe not. Maybe there are nine kinds. Ten. Twelve.
    But eight feels — like an octave. Complete in a different way.
    Seven unique + one transitional.
    
    Let it be eight for now.
```
